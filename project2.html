First excavations

First campaign; between 1990-2005, coordinated by the Regional Archaeological Museum of Banyoles and the Centre for Underwater Archaeology of Catalunya.

Second campaign; between 2008-2015, revealed new sectors besides the main three.

The thing that makes La Draga unique, so the preservation of tools from perishable materials was also a disadvantage during excavations. The variations in the groundwater level have affected the conservation of the Neolithic objects. In dry-land parts, archeologists worked with a diesel-powered water pump to lower the water table in the area under investigation. In other areas, the work was conducted with underwater divers.

Divisions
Nowadays the site is divided in two by a 19th century dike. One part, comprising the Neolithic shoreline, lies beneath the waters of the Lake, but the most extensive area is on dry-land. A; the highest of the three, located in the eastern zone.
The Huts

In La Draga we see rectangular huts with rows of pillars, walls made of branches supporting the double-sloping roof. This style matches the usual patterns for Neolithic lakeside villages, which are well known in the Alpine zone from the middle Neolithic to the Bronze Age. Rectangular wooden huts are the most suitable for lakeside environments.
